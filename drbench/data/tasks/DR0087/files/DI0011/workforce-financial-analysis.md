# Workforce Financial Stability Analysis

## Introduction

As Lee's Market continues to grow and evolve, understanding our workforce's financial health becomes increasingly important. This analysis aims to provide insight into our employees' financial stability, focusing particularly on secondary income sources. The findings can be used to inform our employee benefit offerings and consider potential impacts on job performance and satisfaction.

## Employee Demographics and Financial Stability

Lee's Market has a diverse workforce with employees hailing from various backgrounds. As of 2020, the age distribution ranged from 18 to 65, with the majority of employees falling within the 25-35 age bracket. The company also conducted a financial stability survey in 2019, revealing that 70% of employees felt financially secure.

## Secondary Income Sources among Retail Workers

In the broader retail industry, workers often explore secondary income avenues to supplement their earnings. The most common sources are part-time jobs, freelance gigs, and online selling. In 2018, a study showed that 15% of retail workers across Canada had some form of secondary income.

## Impact of Secondary Income on Job Performance

There is a recurring debate on the impact of secondary income on job performance in the retail sector. Some argue that it can lead to fatigue and decreased productivity, while others believe it promotes resilience and adaptability. However, there is no conclusive evidence to substantiate either claim.

## Conclusion

The analysis shows a significant proportion of our retail workers have secondary income sources. This insight could have implications for job commitment, performance, and overall satisfaction. Moving forward, we need to consider how we can better support our employees’ financial needs and adjust our benefits accordingly. Further research is recommended to understand the specific nature of these secondary incomes and their impact on our employees’ lives.
