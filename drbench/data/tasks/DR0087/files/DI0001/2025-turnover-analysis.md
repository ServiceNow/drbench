# 2025 Employee Turnover Analysis: Lee's Market

## Introduction

This report provides an insight into Lee's Market's employee turnover dynamics during 2025. It encompasses a detailed analysis of voluntary turnover rates, reasons behind separations, and the comparison with the industry average. The information presented is critical for our ongoing human resource strategies and future staffing plans.

## Overview of Employee Separations in 2025

In 2025, Lee's Market experienced a total of 400 employee separations across all departments. The bulk of these separations were concentrated in the retail and purchasing departments, which accounted for 60% of the total. The remaining separations were fairly evenly distributed across other departments such as logistics, administration, and management.

## Analysis of Voluntary Turnover Rates

It's important to analyze the reasons behind voluntary turnover. In 2024, a survey indicated dissatisfaction with leadership was a leading cause, while others cited lack of career advancement opportunities. Addressing these issues could potentially reduce voluntary turnover rates in the future.

## Comparison with Industry Turnover Trends

In 2025, the retail industry saw an average turnover rate of 15%. Smaller retail companies experienced turnover rates as high as 20%, while large-scale retailers saw rates as low as 10%. The high turnover can be attributed to factors such as low wages, lack of benefits, and the physically demanding nature of retail jobs.

## Conclusion

The voluntary turnover rate at Lee's Market in 2025 indicates that there are areas for improvement in employee retention strategies. Comparisons with industry averages suggest that we need to further investigate and address the root causes of these separations. It is recommended to conduct an employee satisfaction survey to identify areas of concern and improve our retention strategies.
