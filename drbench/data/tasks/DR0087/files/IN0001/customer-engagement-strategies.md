# Improving Customer Engagement: A Sales Perspective

## Introduction

In light of the recent retail sales decline in Canada, this report will propose a series of strategies aimed at enhancing on-floor customer engagement in Lee's Market. As a Sales Associate, my observations and experiences place me in a unique position to offer practical insights and recommendations. These proposals are designed to help our company offset the current downturn and drive a measurable rebound in the remaining months of 2025.

## Proposed Enhancements for Customer Interaction

Given the success of our previous initiatives, we are considering introducing self-checkout stations to reduce wait times. Additionally, we are exploring the potential benefits of an in-store loyalty program, which could offer personalized deals based on purchase history. Both of these ideas are projected for implementation in the first quarter of 2026.

## Analysis of Current On-Floor Engagement Methods

A detailed analysis of our on-floor engagement methods reveals a significant concern. The period between May and July 2025 witnessed an alarming 18% reduction in the average time customers spent on our sales floor. This is an indication of a decrease in customer shopping-duration, which is a crucial factor influencing their purchasing decisions. To counteract this trend and drive a measurable sales rebound in the remaining months of 2025, we need to prioritize initiatives that enhance personalized interactions with our customers on the sales floor.

## Monitoring and Measuring Customer Engagement Impact

To assess the impact of our customer engagement efforts, we utilize a range of metrics, including sales figures, customer satisfaction surveys, and in-store foot traffic. For example, in 2024, our customer satisfaction score increased by 12% after the implementation of an interactive in-store map feature.

## Conclusion

By analyzing our current engagement methods and implementing proposed enhancements, Lee's Market can effectively invigorate on-floor customer interaction. Continuous monitoring and measuring of their impact will ensure we stay on the right track. The goal is to not only offset the recent sales decline but also to foster a sustainable engagement model for future growth. It is recommended that these strategies be implemented and evaluated at the earliest opportunity for maximum effect.
