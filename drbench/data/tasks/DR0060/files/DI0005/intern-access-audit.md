# Q1 2024 Intern Access Audit

## Introduction

As part of our ongoing commitment to secure patient data, this report highlights the usage patterns of our clinical communication system during Q1 2024. It specifically focuses on the behavior of our newly onboarded interns. The objective is to better understand their system utilization and to identify any potential security concerns.

## Overview of Intern Onboarding Process

At MediConn, our intern onboarding process begins with an initial orientation on the first day, followed by a week of training. This includes hands-on practice with our virtual healthcare software, which is carried out under the supervision of experienced team leaders. The training week culminates in a final assessment to ensure interns are fully equipped to handle the responsibilities of their role.

## Analysis of System Access Patterns

In 2023, we observed a notable increase in system access, particularly in the customer support department, which recorded a 20% rise in usage frequency. This trend likely correlates with our growing customer base and the increased use of our virtual health services. A detailed analysis will be conducted to optimize our system architecture and improve response times.

## Intern Interaction with Clinical Communication System

Interns are vital to our operations and are given access to the Clinical Communication System under careful supervision. They primarily use it for patient follow-up and consultations. In Q2 2024, we plan to expand this access to include additional systems related to data analytics and reporting.

## Conclusion

The report provides a comprehensive outlook on the usage patterns of the clinical communication system by our new interns. It is crucial to continue monitoring these patterns for any potential security threats. The onboarding process may need to be revised to ensure new interns are properly educated about data security. Further recommendations will be made upon a more detailed analysis.
