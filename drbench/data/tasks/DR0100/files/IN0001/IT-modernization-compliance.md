# IT Modernization for Virtual Care Compliance

## Introduction

To maintain our competitive advantage in the virtual healthcare market, it is crucial that MediConn Solutions aligns its IT infrastructure with evolving national virtual care standards. This document outlines key areas of focus for our IT modernization efforts in anticipation of the shared digital health infrastructure projected for 2026. Our aim is to ensure we are well-positioned to meet these standards while enhancing our service delivery capabilities.

## Analysis of Current National Virtual Care Standards

As we stand at the end of Q4 2025, our examination into the alignment of MediConn’s platform with the Pan-Canadian Virtual Care Policy standards reveals that our compliancy rate is 60%. This figure is indicative of our phased approach towards the adoption of privacy and equity standards outlined in the federal virtual-care framework. Our strategic focus on these areas is reflective of our endeavor to be at the forefront of regulatory compliance within the virtual/remote healthcare industry. As we move towards 2026, it is imperative that we continue to prioritize these efforts to ensure complete compliance with the national virtual care standards.

## Projected Evolution of Shared Digital Health Infrastructure

The digital health infrastructure is projected to grow at a CAGR of 6% over the next four years, driven by advancements in cloud computing and artificial intelligence. However, challenges persist, such as inconsistent data standards among healthcare providers and the lack of a unified patient identification system. These may impede the seamless sharing of patient data in virtual care scenarios.

## Mapping IT Modernization Initiatives to Future Standards

MediConn Solutions has always been proactive in adopting and integrating new technologies into its infrastructure. In 2020, we migrated 70% of our data to a new cloud-based system, which has significantly improved our operational efficiency. This year, we are considering the adoption of AI-powered diagnostic tools to improve our virtual care services.

## Conclusion

Ensuring MediConn's IT infrastructure is compliant with national virtual care standards is essential to our continued growth and success.
