# CPPA Act Compliance Preparedness Plan

## Introduction

As the CPPA Act (Bill C-27) awaits parliamentary decision, it's imperative for MediConn to proactively plan for its potential implementation. This document will outline the key areas we need to focus on to ensure a smooth transition should the Act come into effect by Q2 2026. It's our responsibility as the IT department to ensure our systems and processes are compliant and adaptable to new regulations.

## IT Infrastructure Modifications for Compliance

MediConn's IT department has been working tirelessly on various modifications, including a revamp of our server architecture in 2020. This project led to a 20% increase in system efficiency. Additionally, our move to a hybrid cloud model has significantly reduced downtimes, ensuring our services are more reliable for our patients.

## Data Privacy and Security Enhancements

As we near the end of Q4 2025, we have made significant strides in our assessment of MediConn's data processing systems and integrations in relation to the forthcoming CPPA legislation. Our diligent efforts have culminated in the review of approximately 70% of these systems, a testament to our proactive risk mapping strategy. This preliminary alignment with the CPPA principles lays a firm foundation for our journey towards full compliance. Looking ahead, our focus is now on reviewing the remaining 30% of our systems to ensure a seamless transition as we approach the potential enactment of the Act by Q2 2026.

## Employee Training and Awareness Programs

In 2022, MediConn introduced an innovative cyber awareness program that educated 90% of our staff on recognizing and preventing phishing attacks. The program led to a 15% reduction in successful phishing attempts within six months. This initiative is part of our commitment to creating a cyber-resilient workforce.

## Conclusion

In anticipation of the CPPA Act (Bill C-27), it is essential for MediConn to take proactive measures. By enhancing our data privacy and security, modifying our IT infrastructure for compliance, and conducting necessary employee training, we can ensure a smooth transition. The IT department will continue to monitor the progress of the CPPA Act and adjust our strategy as needed. It's our collective responsibility to ensure we are prepared and remain compliant with all regulatory changes.
