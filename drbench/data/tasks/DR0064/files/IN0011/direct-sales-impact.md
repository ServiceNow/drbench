# Direct-to-Customer Sales Model Impact Analysis

## Introduction

As Elexion Automotive transitions towards a complete direct-to-customer sales model by 2026, it's crucial to understand the potential impacts on our sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer across the U.S. This document serves to examine the implications of this shift, particularly in states with franchise laws, and guide us towards strategies that optimize our sales performance.

## Impact of Direct Sales Model on Customer Engagement

Our customer service department has been gearing up for the transition to a direct sales model. They've rolled out a new CRM system to streamline interactions and are in the process of training staff. Initial feedback from a small group of customers indicates increased satisfaction with our streamlined communication channels.

## Assessment of Current Franchise Law Constraints

After rigorous evaluation of Elexion Automotive's infrastructure and strategic plans for the years 2024 and 2025, it has been established that our manufacturing capabilities are well-positioned to facilitate a transition to a production model specifically geared towards direct sales. This transition is projected to be operational by the first quarter of 2026, aligning with our strategic business goals. The shift to a direct sales model not only acknowledges the evolving industry landscape but is also a proactive response to existing franchise laws across the United States.

## Projected Sales Performance Under Direct-to-Customer Model

Our finance department is currently working on financial projections for the direct-to-customer sales model. Preliminary results suggest positive trends in overall sales volume. However, they've also noted potential challenges related to inventory management and fulfillment under the new model.

## Conclusion

The transition to a complete direct-to-customer sales model presents both challenges and opportunities. Our findings indicate potential for a streamlined sales process, improved customer engagement, and higher average revenue per customer, despite franchise law constraints. As we move forward, we must focus on strategies that mitigate legal challenges while maximizing the benefits of this model. Further research and continuous monitoring of our sales performance will be crucial in this transition.
