# DTC Sales Model Impact Analysis

## Introduction

The upcoming adoption of a complete direct-to-customer (DTC) sales model by Elexion Automotive presents a significant change to our current sales processes. This report explores the possible impacts on our sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer across the United States. The analysis takes into account the unique challenges posed by different franchise laws in various states. The intent is to provide insights that will guide us in making informed business decisions.

## Projected Changes in Sales Cycle Duration

In our analysis of the potential impacts of transitioning to a full direct-to-customer sales model, we noted that dealer partnerships in 2024 were adding an additional layer of cost to our vehicles, with an average of a 9% increase over our suggested retail prices. This additional markup not only affects the final price tag for the customer, but also extends the sales cycle as negotiations and dealership logistics come into play. By eliminating this markup through a direct sales model, we could potentially shorten the sales cycle and provide a more streamlined and efficient purchasing experience for our customers. However, the impact on conversion rates and average revenue per customer will need to be closely monitored and evaluated against these potential benefits.

## Forecasted Impact on Conversion Rates

Our sales department has been using advanced CRM systems to track lead conversion rates since 2017. From 2018 to 2020, we noticed a 15% increase in conversion rates, largely attributed to improvements in our marketing strategies. We continually strive to optimize our sales funnel, with a focus on enhancing customer engagement and satisfaction.

## Expected Shifts in Average Revenue per Customer

Elexion Automotive's average revenue per customer in 2020 was $35,000, a 5% increase from the previous year. The growth in revenue is primarily due to the introduction of our premium range electric vehicles. Our sales team has been working diligently to upsell these high-end models to our existing and potential customers.

## Conclusion

The transition to a DTC sales model will likely result in significant changes to our sales cycle, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer. Understanding these potential impacts is crucial for planning and strategizing accordingly. The Sales Operations team should continue to monitor these areas closely as we move forward with the DTC model. Further, we recommend conducting periodic reviews to assess the effectiveness of the new sales model.
