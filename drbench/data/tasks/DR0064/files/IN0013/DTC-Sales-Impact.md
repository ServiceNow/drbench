# Direct-to-Customer Sales Model Impact Analysis

## Introduction

In light of Elexion Automotive's strategic decision to adopt a complete direct-to-customer sales model by 2026, this report aims to assess the potential implications for our sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer across the United States. The analysis is particularly significant considering the varying franchise laws in different states, which might influence the transition. The insights derived from the report will provide a robust foundation for guiding our sales strategies and processes.

## Comparative Analysis of Sales Models

Our analysis of the 2024 data reveals that Elexion Automotive's dealership network was successful in converting 12% of leads into sales. This metric provides a significant baseline as we evaluate the potential implications of transitioning to a direct-to-customer sales model. It is essential to consider this current conversion rate while modeling the anticipated changes in sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer under the new sales structure. As we move forward, the goal will be to surpass this benchmark by leveraging benefits such as increased profitability, as observed in direct-to-customer models like Tesla's.

## Impact on Sales Cycle and Conversion Rates

In general, the trend towards digitization has significantly shortened sales cycles across various industries by streamlining operations. For instance, in the Q2 of 2019, the average online sales cycle was recorded to be 20% shorter than that of offline sales. As for conversion rates, a report from the National Automobile Dealers Association (NADA) suggested a slight increase in online sales conversions compared to traditional dealership sales.

## Revenue Implications and Customer Value Optimization

The automotive industry has seen a shift in revenue sources over the past decade with an increased focus on after-sales services and digital solutions. By 2025, it's projected that around 30% of automotive revenue will be generated from sources beyond the sale of new vehicles, such as maintenance, financing, and digital services. This shift necessitates a reevaluation of customer value optimization strategies to ensure sustained profitability.

## Conclusion

The transition to a direct-to-customer model represents a significant shift in our sales strategy, with potential implications for our sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer. The insights provided in this report should guide our decision-making processes and help us optimize sales strategies during the transition. It is recommended that we closely monitor these metrics and adjust our strategies as necessary to ensure a successful transition and continued growth.
