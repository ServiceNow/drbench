# Direct-to-Customer Sales Model Impact Analysis

## Introduction

As Elexion Automotive prepares to fully transition to a direct-to-customer sales model by 2026, it is critical to understand the potential impacts on our sales operations. This report will explore potential changes to sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer, with a particular focus on the implications of various U.S. franchise laws. The insights presented herein are based on comprehensive data analysis and will aid in making strategic decisions.

## Evolution of the Automotive Sales Model

As we continue to evolve our business strategies at Elexion Automotive, the feasibility of adopting a direct-to-customer sales model has been thoroughly assessed. However, it should be noted that all of our current dealer partnership agreements hold validity beyond the close of December 2025. This contractual extension presents a significant consideration in terms of our sales model transformation timeline and its potential implications on sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer.

## Implications of Franchise Laws on Direct Sales

Franchise laws in the automotive industry regulate the relationship between manufacturers and dealerships. They were initially enacted in the 1950s to protect dealerships from the market power of manufacturers. These laws have been revised over time, but they still restrict manufacturers from selling directly to consumers in many states, thus preserving the role of dealerships in the automotive supply chain.

## Projected Sales Performance Metrics Post-Implementation

Following the implementation of our direct sales model, the sales department anticipates a potential uptick in overall sales volume. We predict that the number of units sold will increase by 15% in Q3 2026, and we expect this trend to continue into 2027. This projection is based on our internal analysis and the market's current growth trajectory.

## Conclusion

The shift to a direct-to-customer sales model presents both opportunities and challenges for Elexion Automotive. While we anticipate potential improvements in sales cycle duration, conversion rates, and average revenue per customer, the impact of U.S. franchise laws should not be underestimated. Moving forward, we must carefully consider our strategies to overcome potential legal barriers. Detailed action plans will be developed based on these findings to ensure a smooth transition and maintain our strong market position.
