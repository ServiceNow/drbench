# Cybersecurity Incident Review FY 2025

## Introduction

As part of our commitment to maintaining robust cybersecurity measures, this document presents an overview of cybersecurity incidents handled by the Elexion Automotive cybersecurity team in fiscal year 2025. The report also provides insights into the nature of these threats and how they were addressed. This data should guide our strategic planning for enhancing our cybersecurity infrastructure and protocols, ensuring the protection of our systems and customer data.

## Overview of Cybersecurity Threat Landscape

The global cybersecurity landscape continues to evolve at an unprecedented pace. In 2024, according to industry reports, there was a 15% increase in cyberattacks targeting the automotive sector. The most common types of attacks were ransomware and phishing schemes, with an increased focus on compromising connected vehicle systems.

## Incident Response and Resolution Strategies

Elexion Automotive's cybersecurity team utilizes a proactive, four-step incident response strategy: preparation, detection and analysis, containment, and post-incident analysis. The team has invested in advanced threat detection systems and engages in regular simulation exercises to ensure readiness. Additionally, they work closely with all departments to promote cybersecurity awareness and preparedness.

## Cybersecurity Incident Metrics and Analysis

In 2024, Elexion Automotive began leveraging advanced analytics to measure and improve the effectiveness of its cybersecurity program. Key performance indicators include system vulnerability detection rates, average time to respond to incidents, and the efficiency of recovery processes. These metrics provide valuable insights to continuously improve Elexion's cybersecurity efforts.

## Conclusion

The data presented herein underscores the importance of continued vigilance and investment in our cybersecurity infrastructure. The increasing complexity of threats demands preemptive strategies and constant system updates. We recommend increased budget allocation for cybersecurity operations and further staff training. Continued monitoring and regular reporting will be pivotal in mitigating future threats.
