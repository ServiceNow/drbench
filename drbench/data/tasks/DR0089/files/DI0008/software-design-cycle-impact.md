# Impact of Software Dependency on Design Cycles

## Introduction

This report aims to analyze the influence of software dependency on the vehicle design cycles at Elexion Automotive in 2025. As the industry increasingly leans towards the integration of software in automobile design and functionality, understanding this correlation becomes crucial. The insights derived may be instrumental in driving future design strategies and maximizing efficiency.

## Evolution of Vehicle Design Cycles

The design cycles of electric vehicles have seen a considerable evolution over the last decade. From 2010 to 2020, the average cycle length increased from 18 to 24 months. This was largely a result of the growing complexity of electric vehicle technology and the need for extensive safety and performance testing.

## Integration of Software in Automotive Design

Elexion Automotive incorporated software into their design process in the early 2010s. By 2017, 60% of our vehicles' functions were controlled by on-board software. This transition has led to new challenges and opportunities in the design and manufacturing of our vehicles.

## Software Dependency and Design Cycle Efficiency

In the context of design cycles, software dependency has both pros and cons. On one hand, it allows for greater customization and adaptability in vehicle design. On the other, it can introduce additional complexity and potential for delays in the design process.

## Conclusion

The analysis elucidates the correlation between software dependency and the duration of vehicle design cycles. It appears that the increasing software integration has significantly influenced our design processes. It is recommended to further invest in software platforms that could streamline our design cycles and boost efficiency. This would ensure Elexion Automotive stays competitive in the fast-evolving electric vehicles market.
