# Virtual Healthcare Patient Demographics Analysis

## Introduction

As part of our ongoing efforts to optimize our virtual healthcare services, we have conducted an in-depth analysis of our patient demographic trends. The purpose of this report is to present key findings, focusing on the proportion of first-time patients in our virtual consultations. Understanding these dynamics will allow us to identify growth opportunities and improve our patient care strategies.

## Trends in Patient Onboarding

As of 2024, we have observed a 20% increase in the use of our online patient onboarding tools. The adoption of digital health records and the integration of these into our virtual healthcare platform has streamlined the onboarding process. Moreover, we have implemented a new knowledge-based authentication system to further secure patient data during the onboarding process.

## Demographic Profile of First-Time Patients

First-time patients at MediConn Solutions are typically aged between 30 and 50 years old. This age group represents approximately 60% of our new patient demographic. Interestingly, we have noticed a growing number of first-time patients from rural areas, likely due to increased accessibility to internet services in these locations.

## Impact of First-Time Patients on Service Utilization

First-time patients have significantly contributed to the increased utilization of our mental health services. As of the last quarter of 2024, there has been an increase of 15% in the use of mental health services by new patients. However, the usage of other services remains relatively stable.

## Conclusion

The data presented in this report provides valuable insights into our patient population, particularly the high proportion of first-time users of our virtual consultation services. As we move forward, it will be crucial to continue monitoring these trends and adjust our strategies accordingly. Our focus should be on retaining these new users and ensuring their transition to recurrent patients. Further research is recommended to understand the specific needs and preferences of this patient demographic.
