# IT Request Resolution Analysis Report

## Introduction

As MediConn Solutions continues to scale and innovate in the virtual healthcare space, the role of our IT infrastructure becomes increasingly crucial. This report provides an in-depth analysis of the resolution times for IT requests, focusing primarily on non-security related issues. This information is crucial for our strategic planning as it aids in identifying areas where we can improve our efficiency and responsiveness. The data analyzed for this report is extracted from our internal IT service management system.

## Overall IT Service Request Volume and Trends

In 2023, MediConn Solutions handled over 10,000 IT service requests, a 15% increase from 2022. The majority of these requests came from the customer service department, reflecting the increased reliance on digital platforms for patient consultations. This trend aligns with the broader industry shift towards virtual healthcare services.

## Detailed Breakdown of Non-Security IT Request Types

The non-security IT requests at MediConn Solutions are largely related to system updates and software glitches. Approximately 60% of these requests were for system updates, while the remaining 40% were to address software glitches. The finance and human resources departments had the highest volume of these requests.

## Analysis of Resolution Times for Non-Security IT Requests

The resolution times for non-security IT requests vary depending on the nature and complexity of the issue. Simple issues such as password resets are typically resolved within minutes, while complex issues like system updates can take several hours. The IT department is continually working to improve these times.

## Conclusion

The data presented in this report provides valuable insights into our IT request resolution process. While the volume of requests remains manageable, there are clear opportunities to improve our efficiency in handling non-security related requests. Moving forward, it is recommended that we explore solutions to streamline this aspect of our IT operations. This could involve investing in additional resources, implementing new technologies, or refining our existing processes.
