# Facilities Management Report: Lee's Market NYC

## Introduction

This report provides a comprehensive overview of the facilities management within Lee's Market's New York City location. It includes an assessment of the current waste management system, layout, and overall cleanliness. The document will serve as a reference for the ongoing improvement of our store environment and operation efficiency. It is crucial to align our facilities management with our commitment to providing a superior shopping experience.

## Waste Management System Overview

Lee's Market utilizes an advanced waste management system to manage and dispose of waste products efficiently. All wastes are sorted into three main categories: biodegradable, non-biodegradable, and hazardous materials. This is done to ensure that all waste is disposed of in an environmentally friendly manner. The waste management system was last updated in Q1 2025 to meet the latest environmental regulations set by the government.

## Store Layout and Fixture Inventory

Our stores are designed to facilitate easy navigation and accessibility for all customers. In Q4 2025, the average Lee's Market store contains approximately 15 aisles, 100 shelves, and 10 check-out counters. The fixture inventory is regularly updated to ensure that all fixtures are in good condition and meet our high standards of quality and safety. The last fixture inventory check was conducted in Q3 2025.

## Store Cleanliness and Maintenance Protocol

Maintaining a clean and safe environment in our stores is a top priority for Lee's Market. Our maintenance team conducts a comprehensive cleaning and maintenance check every day after store hours. All areas, including the floors, shelves, and checkout counters, are thoroughly cleaned and sanitized. Our maintenance protocol was last updated in Q2 2025 to comply with the new health and safety regulations.

## Conclusion

In summary, the store's waste management system is efficient, with the current number of trash cans adequately serving customer needs. The store layout is carefully designed to maximize customer flow and the maintenance protocol ensures a clean and inviting environment. It is recommended that we continue to monitor and adjust these aspects as necessary to ensure optimal retail conditions.
