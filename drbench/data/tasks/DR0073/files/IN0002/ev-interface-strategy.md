# Interface Design Strategy: Enhancing EV Control Systems

## Introduction

As Elexion Automotive continues to advance in the electric vehicle market, we must also evolve our user interface design strategy. Particularly, our focus is set on reducing the control/display problem rate in our EVs, aiming to surpass the industry's average PP100 scores. This document outlines our strategic approach to prioritize physical buttons and switches for the 2026 EV models, to be implemented by Q3 2025.

## Analysis of EV Control/Display Problem Rate

In the latter part of 2024, our Quality and Reliability Department conducted a rigorous assessment of prototype models featuring physical rotary knobs for key controls. The results were promising, with a significant 82% of test drivers reporting enhanced task accuracy when using these models instead of ones based on touchscreen sliders. These findings highlight the potential advantages of integrating more tactile elements into our control interface designs. Alongside other quality improvements, this strategy could help us to reduce our control/display problem rate, which is currently 30% higher than the industry average.

## Impact of Interface Design on Vehicle Quality

An EV's interface design plays a substantial role in overall vehicle quality. Over the last two years, we have seen a 15% rise in customer satisfaction scores when we improved the user interface design. This shows a clear correlation between the interface design and customer perception of vehicle quality. However, the specific elements leading to this increase are still under investigation.

## Implementation Plan for Revised Control Interface

Our team has proposed a roadmap for implementing a revised control interface. The first stage is the conceptual design phase, which we aim to complete by the end of Q1 2025. Subsequent stages include prototyping, testing, and final implementation, which we project to be ready by Q1 2026. This timeline allows for thorough testing and quality control procedures to ensure a seamless transition for our customers.

## Conclusion

Through a robust analysis and a comprehensive implementation strategy, we are well-positioned to reduce our EV control/display problem rate significantly. Prioritizing physical buttons and switches in our interface design will enhance user experience and improve our PP100 scores. As we move forward, it's essential to maintain cross-functional collaboration to ensure the successful rollout of our new controls interface. Let's drive Elexion Automotive towards a sustainable, user-centric, and quality-focused future.
