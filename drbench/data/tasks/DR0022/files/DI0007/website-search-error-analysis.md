# Website Search Error Analysis - August 2025

## Introduction

As part of our ongoing commitment to provide an optimal online shopping experience, an analysis was conducted to ascertain the performance of our website search function in August 2025. This report will outline the key findings related to search system errors and their impact on product categories. The goal is to gain insights and identify opportunities for improving customer experience and site usability.

## Search System Performance Overview

In July 2025, the search system records showed an uptime of 99.8%, a slight dip from June's 99.9%, with the most significant downtime occurring on July 15th due to a server maintenance issue. The average response time remained fairly consistent at around 0.8 seconds. The Operations and IT team have been working rigorously to maintain and further improve these performance metrics.

## Impacted Product Categories Analysis

Our analysis in Q3 of 2025 revealed that the seafood and fresh produce sections were the most popular categories among our customers, resulting in higher traffic and potential system stress. We also observed an increase in the demand for Korean and Japanese food products, reflecting a shift in consumer food preferences.

## Autocomplete Functionality Evaluation

In Q2 2025, we introduced an improved autocomplete function designed to enhance user experience by providing relevant suggestions faster. Post-implementation, user feedback was primarily positive, with a noted increase in search completion rates. The IT department is continuously monitoring the functionality for further improvements.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, it is evident that there were issues with our search system that predominantly affected a specific product category in August 2025. These issues have had noticeable impact on customer experience and potential sales. It is recommended that we prioritize fixing these errors, with special attention to the autocomplete functionality, to enhance our online shopping experience. We must also ensure that we continue to monitor and evaluate the system regularly to prevent similar issues in the future.
