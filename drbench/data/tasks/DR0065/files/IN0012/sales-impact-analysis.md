# Direct-to-Customer Model: Sales Impact Analysis

## Introduction

As Elexion Automotive contemplates shifting from dealership franchising to a direct-to-customer sales model, we must thoroughly evaluate the potential benefits and challenges. This report will provide an in-depth analysis of the potential implications of this transition on our sales and margins. We will also explore the strategic adjustments necessary to leverage the new model effectively. The insights derived will guide our decision-making process and strategy development.

## Assessment of Current Dealership Franchising Model

In our analysis of the potential shift from the dealership franchising model to a direct-to-customer sales approach, we have identified a compelling customer incentive to encourage online purchases. Our proposal, which aligns with our 2026 transition timeline, is a strategic partnership with a national charging network provider. This partnership would allow us to offer complimentary charging as a unique benefit for customers who choose to make their purchase directly from our online platform.

## Potential Benefits and Challenges of Direct-to-Customer Model

The direct-to-customer sales model would necessitate significant changes to our supply chain operations and IT infrastructure. A bespoke e-commerce platform would need to be developed, and our logistics team would need to manage more complex distribution routes. The customer service department would also have to scale up to handle increased direct interactions with customers.

## Strategic Adjustments for Effective Transition

To effectively transition to a direct-to-customer model, strategic alignment across all departments will be crucial. For instance, our IT team will need to work closely with Finance to budget for the necessary technological investments. Meanwhile, our HR department will need to plan for hiring and training staff to support the new sales model.

## Conclusion

The transition to a direct-to-customer model could potentially unlock new growth opportunities for Elexion Automotive. However, careful consideration must be given to the challenges and necessary strategic adjustments. The insights derived from this analysis will inform our decision-making process and guide our strategies going forward. It is essential that we approach this transition in a well-informed and strategic manner to maximize potential benefits and mitigate risks.
