# Direct-to-Customer Sales Strategy 2027

## Introduction

As Elexion Automotive considers transitioning from dealership franchising to a direct-to-customer sales model, it's crucial to understand the potential benefits and implications. This document will explore the areas of impact and provide strategic insights to maximize the potential advantages. We will focus on sales processes, customer relationship management, and the financial viability of this transition. Our goal is to provide a comprehensive analysis to inform our decision-making and strategic planning.

## CRM Adaptation for Direct Sales

The CRM system was initially implemented in 2019 and since then we have witnessed a considerable reduction in customer churn rate by 8%. The system has been instrumental in maintaining a comprehensive customer database and managing customer interactions effectively.

## Sales Process Transformation

Our analysis of the sales figures from June 2024 reveals that the average profit margin per unit achieved through our dealership network stood at approximately $4,200. This figure, albeit significant, might be substantially improved with a shift to a direct-to-customer sales strategy. By eliminating intermediary costs and dealership markups, we can potentially increase our per-unit profit margin, thus driving higher overall revenues. Moreover, the adoption of a direct sales model would give us greater control over the customer experience, further fostering brand loyalty and, in turn, potentially leading to increased sales volume.

## Financial Impact and Margin Optimization

In Q3 of 2024, we undertook a margin optimization project that led to an 11% increase in gross profit by reducing material costs. We also managed to save an additional 2% on production costs by streamlining our assembly line process.

## Conclusion

The transition to a direct-to-customer sales model presents both challenges and opportunities for Elexion Automotive. A thorough understanding of the sales process transformation, CRM adaptation, and financial impact is key to capturing the benefits of this transition. We recommend initiating a pilot project to test the new sales model before full implementation. Further detailed analysis and planning are required to ensure a successful transition.
