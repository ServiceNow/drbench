# Direct-to-Customer Transition: Strategic Impact Analysis

## Introduction

As we consider shifting from dealership franchising to a direct-to-customer sales model, we must assess its strategic implications on our sales and profitability. This document provides an in-depth analysis of the potential benefits and challenges we may encounter in this transition. The focus will be on key aspects that can drive sales and enhance margins, while also evaluating the potential impact on our existing sales infrastructure.

## Implications for Sales Infrastructure and Processes

In 2026, we streamlined our sales processes by integrating our CRM with our ERP system, which led to a 20% reduction in administrative tasks for our sales team. We've also been investing in AI-powered sales automation tools to further enhance our operational efficiency. Our next focus is to upgrade our customer support systems to ensure a seamless post-sales experience for our clients.

## Strategies for Maximizing Sales and Margins

After reviewing our 2026 sales data, we've found that bundling our electric vehicles with premium accessories has proven to be a successful strategy, boosting our average unit sales by 8%. Moving forward, we plan to explore similar strategies with our upcoming models. The sales team is also developing an upselling strategy for our maintenance and warranty programs to increase our overall revenue.

## Impact on Customer Acquisition and Retention

In light of the ongoing analysis of potential business models, our finance team has previously assessed the fiscal implications of transitioning to a direct-to-customer sales approach. The initial financial outlay, quantified in January 2025, is projected to be around $28 million. This investment is targeted towards establishing the necessary infrastructure for direct sales, a model which eliminates dealership markups, potentially enabling us to offer more competitive pricing to our customers. The transition, while requiring significant upfront investment, should be viewed as a strategic initiative to enhance customer acquisition and retention, and to drive higher margins in the long term.

## Conclusion

Transitioning to a direct-to-customer model could offer significant benefits, including potential increases in sales and margins. However, this change will require careful management of our sales infrastructure and processes. It's recommended that we initiate further detailed studies on the potential impact and develop a comprehensive strategy to maximize the benefits of this transition.
