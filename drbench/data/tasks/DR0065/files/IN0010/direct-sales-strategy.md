# Direct Sales Transition: Strategy and Impact

## Introduction

As Elexion Automotive explores the shift from dealership franchising to a direct-to-customer sales model, understanding potential benefits and implications is critical. This document will examine the anticipated impact of such a transition on our sales operations, profit margins, and broader market strategy. We will also delve into how the change might drive sales and deliver enhanced value in 2027.

## Implications for Profit Margin and Cost Structure

Moving to a direct sales model could alter our cost structure. The elimination of dealership commissions could result in savings, but the burden of providing after-sales service would fall on us. In 2022, our after-sales service costs constituted about 15% of the total expenditure, a figure that could potentially rise. Also, expenses related to technology enhancements and logistics could impact the cost structure.

## Direct Sales Model: Operational Considerations

Our 2024 customer feedback analysis reveals a significant level of dissatisfaction among our target demographics towards the conventional dealership negotiation process. This sentiment underscores the potential benefits of transitioning to a direct-to-consumer sales model, eliminating the frustration associated with dealership markups and negotiation complexities. By adopting this model, Elexion Automotive could bypass these consumer pain points, potentially leading to increased sales and higher profit margins. This approach aligns with current market trends, where leading automotive companies have been successful in implementing direct sales models.

## Leveraging Direct Sales for Market Differentiation

Direct sales could offer us a unique market positioning. It allows us to control the entire customer journey, from research to purchase and after-sales service. In 2025, when we introduced in-car entertainment systems, it helped differentiate us from competitors. Similarly, a direct sales approach could enhance our brand image and provide a unique selling proposition.

## Conclusion

Transitioning to a direct-to-customer sales model could lead to significant operational changes, impact our profit margins, and provide opportunities for market differentiation. We must carefully analyze and plan for these changes to ensure business continuity and capitalize on potential growth opportunities. The sales operations team will play a crucial role in this process, and further analysis is needed to formulate a detailed implementation strategy.
