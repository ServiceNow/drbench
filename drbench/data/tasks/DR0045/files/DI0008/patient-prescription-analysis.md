# Patient Segmentation & Prescription Service Analysis

## Introduction

As MediConn Solutions continues to grow and evolve, understanding our patient segmentation is crucial for strategic planning. This report delves into the demographics and behaviors of our patient base, with a focus on their usage of our prescription service. It aims to identify the most valuable patient groups, thereby informing our marketing and product development strategies.

## Demographic Analysis of Prescription Service Users

Our prescription service has seen a consistent user base across various demographics. In the third quarter of 2023, approximately 45% of our users were between ages 30-50, while 30% were aged 60 and above. Our user base also exhibited a near-equal split between genders, with males representing 52% of users and females making up the remaining 48%.

## Patient Engagement With Prescription Service

Engagement with our prescription service has been steadily increasing. In Q3 2023, we noticed an average of 15% uptick in user engagement, especially within our mobile platform. This is mainly attributed to the new features and user-friendly interface we introduced in our digital platforms in early 2023.

## Revenue Contribution of Patient Segments

Our revenue distribution among patient segments has remained relatively consistent. In Q2 2023, our business-to-business (B2B) segment contributed 60% of the total revenue, with the remaining 40% coming from individual consumers. There was a minor increase in revenue from the B2B sector compared to Q1 2023.

## Conclusion

Our data insights underscore the importance of targeted marketing and tailored service offerings to our most valuable patient segments. These findings should guide our future initiatives in ensuring the continued growth and success of our prescription service. Furthermore, we recommend a continuous review of patient segmentation to adapt to changing patient needs and behaviors. Let's seize this opportunity to provide the best possible care to our most valued patients.
