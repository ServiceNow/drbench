# Quarterly Asset Depreciation Audit Report

## Introduction

This document presents the findings from the recent quarterly asset depreciation audit led by Olivia Martinez. The focus of this audit was on high-value assembly machines, which play a crucial part in Elexion Automotive's manufacturing process. The report provides an overview of the revaluation process and its impact on our assets. It is intended to inform internal stakeholders about the current state of our high-value assets and guide future investment decisions.

## Overview of Asset Depreciation Process

Asset depreciation is a key aspect of our financial management at Elexion Automotive. We follow a straight-line depreciation method, which involves reducing the value of an asset equally over its useful lifespan. The lifespan of our assembly machines is typically determined by our engineering department, which takes into account factors like wear and tear, technological obsolescence, and efficiency. This process is crucial for accurate financial reporting and tax purposes.

## Impact of Asset Revaluation on Financial Statements

Asset revaluation can significantly impact our financial statements. An increase in asset value can enhance the company's net worth on the balance sheet. However, it also means higher annual depreciation costs which can impact our income statement. We ensure to carefully consider these implications before proceeding with any revaluation.

## Summary of High-Value Assembly Machines Revaluation

Elexion Automotive frequently evaluates the need for revaluation of its high-value assembly machines. As of 2020, we have conducted five major revaluations, each time adjusting to changing market conditions and technological advancements. These revaluations are typically overseen by our internal finance team, in conjunction with external auditors.

## Conclusion

The audit's findings indicate a significant impact on our financial statements due to the revaluation of high-value assembly machines. As we continue to invest in our manufacturing capabilities, it is essential to regularly assess the value of our assets. Recommendations for the next quarter include a focus on maintaining our high-value machines to minimize depreciation. Future audits should also expand to cover other critical assets in the company.
