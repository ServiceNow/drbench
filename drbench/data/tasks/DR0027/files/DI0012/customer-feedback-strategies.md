# Customer Feedback Strategies at Lee's Market

## Introduction

This document reviews the various mechanisms we have employed at Lee's Market to collect customer feedback, with a focus on the historical perspective. Understanding the evolution of our customer feedback collection strategies is key to informing our future policies and practices. In this report, we will scrutinize the use of physical suggestion boxes, digital feedback forms, and customer surveys conducted from 2010 to 2015.

## Overview of Customer Feedback Methods

At Lee's Market, we understand the importance of customer feedback. Various methods are employed to gather customer insights, including in-store observations and online surveys. In 2017, we introduced a customer loyalty program that offered customers the chance to provide direct feedback and earn rewards. This initiative increased our customer feedback by 25% in the first quarter.

## Studying the Efficacy of Physical Suggestion Boxes

Lee's Market constantly assesses the effectiveness of our feedback channels. Our research in 2018 showed that physical suggestion boxes were more effective in stores with higher foot traffic. However, in our smaller branches, digital feedback methods proved to be more effective. This led to a 15% increase in overall customer feedback.

## Digital Feedback Channels and Customer Surveys

With the rise of digital technology, Lee's Market has been investing more in digital feedback channels. In 2016, we launched a mobile app, significantly increasing the accessibility of customer feedback. Online surveys conducted via email and social media platforms have also been instrumental in gathering customer insights, seeing a 30% response rate in the first six months of implementation.

## Conclusion

The history of our customer feedback strategies shows an evolution towards digital channels, while retaining some traditional methods. The physical suggestion boxes have played a significant role in our customer feedback collection. Going forward, we need to balance maintaining these traditional methods with the growing demand for digital interaction. The insights gained from this analysis should guide our decisions on future customer feedback strategies.
