# Joint KM and HR Onboarding Improvements 2023

## Introduction

This document provides an overview of the collaborative projects undertaken by the Knowledge Management (KM) and Human Resources (HR) teams at Lee's Market in 2023. The focus is on initiatives aimed at improving the employee onboarding process. The aim is to provide insights into the efficacy and impact of these projects on our growing workforce. The document should serve as a reference for future collaboration between these departments.

## Evolution of Onboarding Process at Lee's Market

The onboarding process at Lee's Market has evolved significantly since the company's inception. Back in 2010, most of the onboarding was conducted in person, with a limited digital presence. By 2018, the company had transitioned to a hybrid model, blending in-person and online onboarding tools to cater to a diverse workforce. In 2022, about 80% of the onboarding activities were carried out online, reflecting the shift towards digitalization.

## Collaborative Initiatives of KM and HR Teams

The Knowledge Management (KM) and Human Resources (HR) teams at Lee's Market have a rich history of collaboration. In 2021, they launched an initiative to redesign the company's internal knowledge sharing platform. Then, in 2022, they partnered to create a series of training programs aimed at improving customer service. These initiatives have fostered a culture of collaboration and knowledge sharing across the enterprise.

## Impact and Outcome of 2023 Onboarding Improvements

The onboarding improvements introduced in 2023 have had a significant impact on employee engagement and retention rates. Within the first six months, the company recorded a 15% increase in employee engagement scores. Furthermore, the turnover rate during the probationary period decreased by 10%. These results underscore the importance of a comprehensive and effective onboarding process.

## Conclusion

The collaboration between the KM and HR teams on employee onboarding improvements in 2023 has led to significant enhancements in our process. It is clear that these initiatives have been beneficial for Lee's Market and its employees. Moving forward, we should continue to foster such interdepartmental collaborations for organizational improvements. Further, we recommend a comprehensive review of the onboarding process every two years to ensure its continued relevance and efficiency.
