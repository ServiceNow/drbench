# Improving Patient Communication for Service Efficiency

## Introduction

As MediConn Solutions continues to expand, enhancing patient communication has become a significant priority. This document explores strategies to improve our communication methods, aiming to reduce service calls by the first quarter of 2025. These strategies align with our commitment to providing user-friendly and efficient healthcare services remotely.

## Current State of Patient Communication

Our recent patient questionnaire from October 2024 has uncovered a critical area for improvement in our customer service department. We found that patients who required additional clarification on their billing post-visit reported lower satisfaction scores, averaging at 3.2 out of 5. However, it's noteworthy that when these billing explanations were offered, the satisfaction scores saw a significant increase to an average of 4.1 out of 5. Comparatively, patients who didn't need post-visit clarification reported high satisfaction levels, averaging at 4.5 out of 5.

## Integration of Advanced Technology for Patient Communication

In 2023, we implemented a new patient portal, which allowed our patients to access their medical records and book appointments online. The portal also facilitated better communication between healthcare professionals and patients. During the first quarter after its introduction, 75% of our patients registered and have actively been using the portal, indicating a high degree of acceptance and satisfaction.

## Potential Impact on Customer Service Efficiency

The customer service department has been working diligently to reduce waiting times on service calls. Last year, we managed to reduce the average waiting time from 7 minutes to 5 minutes, which resulted in an 8% increase in customer satisfaction scores. Our team is now focusing on reducing the number of unresolved calls during the first contact.

## Conclusion

Enhancing patient communication will not only improve our customer service efficiency but also strengthen our commitment to patient-centric care. The integration of advanced technology in our communication strategy is a promising approach. We should consider piloting these improvements in Q4 2024 to measure impacts and adjust strategies accordingly.
