# Media Monitoring Capacity Assessment

## Introduction

This document serves to present an evaluation of our public relations team's capacity in media monitoring. As Lee's Market continues to expand its presence in urban centers across the U.S. and Canada, maintaining vigilant oversight on our media coverage becomes increasingly crucial for our reputation management and brand positioning.

## Public Relations Team Structure and Responsibilities

The Public Relations team at Lee's Market, led by esteemed industry veteran Jennifer Chua, is comprised of 25 individuals. This team is divided into several sub-teams focusing on different areas of public relations, including crisis management, brand reputation, and community relations. In 2020, the team was assigned a specific project to create a promotional campaign for the newly launched online store.

## Media Monitoring Tools and Processes

Lee’s Market utilizes state-of-the-art media monitoring tools to keep a pulse on their brand reputation. These tools are integrated with our CRM system to provide real-time updates and insights. In 2021, these tools contributed to a 15% increase in brand sentiment scores among the 18-30 year demographic.

## Analysis of Current Media Monitoring Capacity

As of the third quarter of 2023, the media monitoring team has been prioritizing the analysis of social media trends and customer sentiment. They have been instrumental in identifying key influencers for collaborations, leading to a 20% increase in Instagram followers. Furthermore, they have been actively involved in monitoring and mitigating potential PR crises.

## Conclusion

Our public relations team's media monitoring capacity is instrumental to our brand's reputation in the rapidly evolving retail landscape. As highlighted, certain areas require improvement and investment. It is recommended that we invest in more sophisticated media monitoring tools and consider team expansion for more comprehensive coverage. This will ensure we continue to maintain our brand's image and address any potential media issues promptly.
