# Community Partnership ROI Analysis 2022-2023

## Introduction

As part of our ongoing efforts to maximize the value and reputation of Lee's Market, we have conducted an in-depth study into the return on investment (ROI) from community partnership programs. The analysis focused on regional food retailers with similar annual revenues to ours, during the period of 2022-2023. This report will present our findings, including the media coverage value, customer acquisition costs, and overall brand sentiment improvements resulting from these partnerships.

## Cost Analysis for New Customer Acquisition

We've implemented a new CRM system in the first quarter of 2021 to enhance our customer segmentation and targeting. As a result, our marketing department reported a 20% decrease in the cost per lead and a 15% increase in conversion rates. This has allowed us to allocate more funds towards expanding our product offerings.

## Evaluation of Media Coverage Value

Our 2022 involvement in the sponsorship of three significant Asian cultural festivals was a strategic investment, yielding fruitful returns. This $150,000 initiative procured a substantial 300% return on investment in the form of earned media. Moreover, it led to a noteworthy 25% upsurge in our acquisition of new, culturally aligned customers. This reflects the value of the brand's commitment to community engagement and the positive impact of our strategic investment in culturally relevant events.

## Brand Sentiment Improvement Metrics

We introduced a customer feedback system in 2020 to measure brand sentiment. Our Q4 2020 report indicated a 10% improvement in customer satisfaction scores compared to Q3. The feedback system continues to provide insights into areas of improvement, shaping our customer service strategies in the following years.

## Conclusion

After thorough analysis, it is apparent that community partnerships can greatly enhance our brand's reputation and customer base. However, the efficiency of these partnerships varies significantly, warranting careful selection and strategic planning. Moving forward, we recommend focusing on partnerships that offer high media value and sentiment improvements while minimizing customer acquisition costs. These findings will inform our future public relations and corporate communication strategies.
