# IT Onboarding & Training Outsourcing Analysis

## Introduction

To further streamline our operations and increase efficiency, we have explored the prospect of outsourcing the onboarding and training of newly-hired IT specialists. This report presents an analysis of the potential time savings for our HR department. The insights provided are based on calculations by our knowledge management team and will help us make informed decisions in our strategic planning for 2026.

## Current HR Time Investment in IT Onboarding & Training

In 2021, the Human Resources department at Lee's Market dedicated approximately 1200 hours to the recruitment and initial training of IT staff. This includes time spent on job postings, interviews, and developing training materials. However, it should be noted that this figure doesn't account for ongoing training and career development programs.

## Potential Outsourcing Providers for IT Onboarding & Training

Several potential providers have been identified for outsourcing IT onboarding and training. These include TechTrain Inc., a company renowned for their comprehensive IT training programs, and OnboardIT, known for their smooth onboarding processes. A full evaluation of these providers will be conducted in Q3 of 2022.

## Projected HR Time Savings through Outsourcing

Our HR department is excited about the potential impact of outsourcing on other areas beyond IT. Preliminary projections indicate that by outsourcing payroll tasks, we can reduce HR's workload by up to 20% in 2023. This will allow our HR team to focus more on strategic initiatives and employee relations.

## Conclusion

The outsourcing of IT specialist onboarding and training could potentially yield significant time savings for our HR department. This would allow HR professionals to refocus their efforts on strategic initiatives, furthering Lee's Market's growth and success. The decision to outsource should be made after carefully considering the implications on our company culture and the quality of training. Next steps should involve engaging potential outsourcing providers to further investigate feasibility and cost implications.
