# Website Banner Engagement Analysis

## Introduction

As part of our continuous efforts to enhance user experience and increase online sales, we've analyzed customer engagement with promotional banners on our homepage. This report provides an in-depth analysis of the promotional banners that are most quickly skipped by customers, potentially indicating areas for improvement in our digital marketing strategies.

## Website Traffic and User Demographics

In January 2021, we noted a 15% increase in website traffic, with a majority of visitors being from urban areas. The age group 24-35 was the dominant demographic, making up 45% of the total user base. Interestingly, our female customers outnumbered the males by a ratio of 3:2.

## Banner Engagement and Customer Behavior Patterns

Over the past year, we've noticed that customers are more likely to engage with our banners between 5 PM and 9 PM. Furthermore, our data shows that customers using mobile devices are 20% more likely to engage with banners than those using desktops. However, the conversion rate is significantly higher among desktop users.

## Impact of Promotional Banners on Sales

Promotional banners have shown to increase sales in the selected product category by an average of 18% during the promotion period. The 'Buy One Get One Free' banner for sushi products in March 2021 saw an impressive 25% boost in sales. However, the '20% off on all noodles' banner in April 2021 only resulted in a 10% increase.

## Conclusion

Understanding customer engagement with our online promotional banners is key to enhancing our digital marketing strategy. The findings in this report suggest areas for improvement to increase customer interaction and ultimately drive sales. Based on these findings, we recommend revisiting our banner design and messaging, with a particular focus on the banners identified as being skipped quickly. Further research may be needed to understand the underlying reasons for these behaviors.
