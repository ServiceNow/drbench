# Q3 Employee Wellness Engagement Report

## Introduction

As part of Elexion Automotive's commitment to the health and wellbeing of our workforce, we have been conducting regular wellness seminars. The third quarter's seminar focused on mental health and stress management. This report provides an overview of the participation rate, the feedback received, and the impact on employee productivity.

## Breakdown of Seminar Participation

In the last quarter, we offered a variety of seminars, not just in the realm of wellness but also in technical skills and leadership. These seminars had a wide range of participation, with the technical skills seminar attracting 55% of the engineering department and the leadership seminar attended by 75% of the managerial staff. Overall, we saw an average participation of 60% across all seminars.

## Employee Feedback on Wellness Seminar

Post-seminar surveys indicated positive reception to our wellness initiative. Over 80% of the participants rated the seminar as 'excellent' or 'good'. The yoga and mindfulness sections were particularly appreciated, with many employees requesting for more such sessions in the future. The keynote speaker from a renowned wellness center was also well received.

## Impact of Wellness Seminar on Employee Productivity

Following the wellness seminar, we have observed an uptick in overall productivity. There has been a 7% increase in productivity metrics for the manufacturing department, and a 5% increase in the engineering department. Additionally, absenteeism rates have reduced by 12% overall, suggesting a positive impact of wellness initiatives on employee health.

## Conclusion

The wellness seminar saw a substantial turnout, indicating employees' interest in health and wellbeing initiatives. Feedback was generally positive, although a few areas for improvement were highlighted. Despite some initial productivity dips, the long-term effects of the seminar on overall productivity appear positive. Moving forward, we recommend continued investment in such initiatives and addressing the identified areas for improvement.
