# February 2025 Customer Service Analysis

## Introduction

This report examines the customer service interactions at Lee's Market during the first half of February 2025. It primarily focuses on cases that required managerial intervention, presenting an overview of the situation across all locations. This analysis will provide insights into the quality of our customer service and inform strategies to enhance customer experience.

## Breakdown of Customer Complaints by Location

In January 2025, Lee's Market in Vancouver reported the highest number of customer complaints, closely followed by our New York City location. Complaints ranged from product dissatisfaction to delivery delays, with the latter accounting for about 65% of total complaints. The Chicago store, on the other hand, recorded the fewest complaints, primarily due to the implementation of our new customer service training program.

## Analysis of Managerial Intervention Cases

In 2024, approximately 55% of customer complaints required managerial intervention across all Lee's Market locations. Most of these cases were related to billing disputes and product refund requests. It's interesting to note that the manager intervention rate decreased by about 10% compared to 2023, indicating improvements in our customer service team's problem-solving abilities.

## Impact of Complaints on Customer Retention

Throughout 2024, there was a 3% decrease in customer retention, partly attributed to unresolved customer complaints. The highest attrition was observed in Q3 2024 when we faced supply chain issues that affected product availability. However, the implementation of our new customer feedback system in Q4 helped mitigate some of the churn.

## Conclusion

The data suggests that while our managerial team is adept at handling escalated complaints, there are certain locations with a higher incidence of such cases. This indicates potential areas for improvement in our customer service training. Moving forward, we recommend further investigation into these locations and implementing targeted training to reduce the number of escalated complaints.
