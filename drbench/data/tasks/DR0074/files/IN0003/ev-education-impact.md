# EV Infotainment Education: Impact on Service Visits

## Introduction

The following report explores the potential ramifications of increasing dealer-led customer education on our Electric Vehicle (EV) infotainment and connectivity features by 20%. As a Quality Assurance Engineer, I aim to identify how this increase can optimize our product reliability and operational efficiency, thereby reducing the frequency of service visits. This analysis considers the existing quality control processes, customer feedback, and service records up to 2027.

## Analysis of EV Infotainment-Related Service Visits

In 2024, a total of 18% of service visits were related to infotainment system issues. Although this was a slight increase from the previous year, we noticed a decrease in the severity of the issues reported. Most of the concerns were related to software updates and minor technical glitches, which were promptly addressed by our service teams.

## Predictive Model: Service Visits Reduction by 2027

Our predictive modeling suggests a general decrease in service visits as our vehicles improve in quality and reliability. By 2027, we expect a 15% reduction in overall service visits compared to 2024 figures. The model considers factors such as anticipated improvements in manufacturing processes, vehicle durability, and customer satisfaction rates.

## Overview of Current Customer Education Efforts

In the early part of 2025, Elexion Automotive made a strategic decision to enhance the understanding and knowledge of its sales and service personnel regarding the increasingly complex infotainment and connectivity systems in our vehicles. This led to the inception of a Dealer Knowledge Certification program, which was specifically designed to cultivate uniform expertise among our team. The objective was to ensure a standardized level of proficiency, enabling our staff to better educate customers and troubleshoot potential issues, thereby enhancing the overall user experience.

## Conclusion

Enhancing customer education on EV infotainment and connectivity features can potentially minimize service visits, elevating customer satisfaction and reducing operational costs. The predictive model indicates a promising trend, but continual monitoring is necessary to ensure its accuracy. Moving forward, it is recommended to align our quality assurance efforts with this initiative for a holistic approach to product reliability. An update on this analysis will be provided on a semi-annual basis.
