# EV Infotainment Education and Service Visit Frequency

## Introduction

As we aim to enhance customer satisfaction and reduce service visit frequency, understanding the potential impacts of increasing dealer-led customer education on EV infotainment and connectivity features becomes crucial. This report explores the relationship between these two variables, providing an analysis of potential effects and collateral impacts. Our goal is to provide data-backed insights that will aid in decision-making and strategic planning, ensuring Elexion Automotive continues to lead in the electric vehicle market.

## Current Trends in EV Infotainment and Connectivity

In 2023, our internal customer satisfaction surveys revealed that a significant portion of our clients, about 41%, encountered challenges when interfacing with their plug-in hybrid and electric vehicles' infotainment systems during the initial 90 days post-purchase. A majority of these issues, specifically 58%, were related to connectivity matters. This feedback underscores the need to enhance user interface design and simplify the connectivity process to improve the overall customer experience.

## Dealer-Led Customer Education: Methods and Impact

In an attempt to enhance the customer's understanding of our vehicles, we've launched a series of dealer-led workshops since January 2024. These workshops cover various topics, including basic EV maintenance, charging infrastructure, and driving efficiency. We've seen a 25% increase in workshop attendance over the past year, indicating a growing interest from our customers.

## Service Visit Frequency and Customer Satisfaction Metrics

Our data from Q3 2024 shows that the average Elexion customer visits an authorized service center approximately twice a year. The most common reasons for these visits include routine maintenance checks and tire replacements. Interestingly, our customer satisfaction survey reveals that 80% of customers feel their service experience met or exceeded their expectations.

## Conclusion

The analysis indicates a potential correlation between increased dealer-led education and reduced service visit frequency. While further research may be required to fully understand the nuances, the data suggests an opportunity to enhance customer experience and reduce service costs. The next steps should involve a detailed implementation strategy for the dealer-led education program. Let's work cross-functionally to ensure a seamless execution that aligns with our commitment to quality and customer satisfaction.
