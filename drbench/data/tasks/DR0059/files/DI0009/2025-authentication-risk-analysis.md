# May 2025 Legacy Authentication Risk Analysis

## Introduction

This report reflects the comprehensive risk-scoring analysis performed on our legacy authentication modules within the MediConn virtual healthcare platform in May 2025. As a cybersecurity specialist, it is critical to continuously evaluate and update our protocols to ensure the security of our patient data. This document will dive into the specifics of the risk analysis, potential vulnerabilities, and recommendations for improvements.

## Authentication Module: Risk Assessment Overview

In Q2 of 2024, an internal audit was performed on our authentication modules in line with industry best practices. The process entailed testing the efficiency of our existing security protocols and benchmarking them against competing virtual healthcare platforms. This comprehensive review allowed us to enhance our security posture and maintain the highest level of data protection to the satisfaction of our 200 business partners.

## Identified Vulnerabilities and Potential Exploits

In the past year, our cybersecurity team has identified and resolved over 50 potential vulnerabilities within our platform's infrastructure. These included minor bugs and loopholes that could potentially be exploited by cybercriminals. Our teams have worked tirelessly to rectify these issues, ensuring continuous service to our 500,000 strong user base.

## Risk Mitigation Strategies and Recommendations

Our team typically recommends a multi-layered security approach to mitigate potential risks. In November 2024, we instituted an additional security layer in the form of two-factor authentication for all users. We believe this step will further secure our platform and give users peace of mind when using our service.

## Conclusion

The conducted risk analysis in May 2025 revealed some vulnerabilities within our legacy authentication modules that require immediate attention. It is recommended that we initiate remedial actions to address these issues and strengthen our cybersecurity framework. Ensuring the security of our virtual healthcare platform is crucial to maintaining the trust of our clientele. Future risk assessments should be planned and conducted regularly to prevent potential security breaches.
