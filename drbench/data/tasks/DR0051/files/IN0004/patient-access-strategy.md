# Enhancing Virtual Patient Access Strategy

## Introduction

As we strive to improve our patient satisfaction and retention rates, it's crucial to assess and enhance our current patient access systems. This document outlines our strategy for Q1 2026, focusing on virtual consultations and prescription management services. We are determined to address the challenges identified in MGMA's findings and capitalize on the opportunities in the evolving digital front door landscape. Our ultimate goal is to reduce wait times and make our services more accessible to our patients.

## Optimization Strategies for Virtual Consultations

In Q3 2025, we initiated a pilot project to explore the feasibility of AI assistance in virtual consultations. The project sought to determine if AI could enhance the patient-doctor interaction by providing real-time data during the consultation. The results of this project are expected by Q2 2026.

## Assessment of Current Patient Access Systems

In our 2024 review, it was found that MediConn Solutions' booking and scheduling systems were a notable area of concern. Despite an overall satisfactory Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 58, our NPS specifically for appointment booking fell significantly to 32. The quarterly patient feedback surveys conducted throughout the year highlighted that nearly half of our detractors pinpointed challenges related to appointment scheduling and excessive wait times as the main drivers of their dissatisfaction. This clearly underscores a pressing need to refine our current systems and processes to better patient experience.

## Revamping Prescription Management Services

In 2025, our prescription management services catered to over 10,000 patients, marking a 30% increase from 2024. Despite the increase in numbers, we’ve managed to maintain an error rate below 2%. Our focus has been on maintaining the accuracy and efficiency of prescription management services.

## Conclusion

By assessing our current systems, optimizing our virtual consultations, and revamping our prescription management services, we can significantly reduce patient wait times and boost satisfaction. These steps will not only enhance our patient access but also promote long-term patient retention. The Director of Customer Service recommends implementing these strategies in Q1 2026. Further discussions and planning will be necessary to ensure effective execution.
