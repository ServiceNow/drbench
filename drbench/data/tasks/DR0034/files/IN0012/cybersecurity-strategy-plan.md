# Cybersecurity Training Enhancement Strategy

## Introduction

As our enterprise expands, so too does our digital footprint and the potential risks associated with it. Given the limited IT resources, we need to optimize our cybersecurity training to better equip our employees to handle potential cyber threats. This document outlines a strategy to strengthen our cybersecurity training program, aiming to significantly reduce the risk of cyber attacks leading to operation disruptions and financial losses by Q3 2027.

## Recommendations for Cybersecurity Training Enhancement

In the light of recent global cyber threats, it's advisable to include modules on advanced persistent threats and ransomware in our future trainings. Additionally, incorporating real-life case studies from retail industry breaches could better illustrate the potential risks. A bi-annual refresher course could also help maintain staff awareness.

## Timeline and Resource Allocation for Implementation

With the proposed changes to our training modules, we aim to roll out the updated curriculum by Q1 2026. Given our current resources, we expect to allocate 20% of the IT department's annual budget to this project. It's essential to note, this will require a shift in resources from other IT projects.

## Current State Assessment of Cybersecurity Training

Our 2024 data review reveals that organizations which experienced no major cyber incidents were those that achieved a high cyber training completion rate, with 95% of their staff fully trained. This metric is vital for Lee's Market, which heavily depends on customer trust and loyalty, which are significantly influenced by our ability to protect customer data and ensure consistent operations. Effective employee training in cybersecurity is a key element of our strategy to maintain our reputation and the trust of our diverse client base. This is critical in an environment where 80% of retailers are targets of cyberattacks and where these attacks can lead to reputational damage and operational downtime.

## Conclusion

By refining our cybersecurity training program, we can better prepare our employees to mitigate cyber threats while utilizing our limited IT resources effectively. The outlined recommendations are based on our current state assessment and are meant to fortify our defenses against potential cyberattacks. The proposed timeline and resource allocation will ensure a structured approach towards the implementation. Let's move forward together to secure our digital landscape and continue to provide our high-quality service to our customers.
