# Strengthening Cybersecurity Training: A Strategic Plan

## Introduction

As our company continues to grow, so does the complexity of our digital landscape. This puts Lee's Market at an increasing risk of cyberattacks. To protect our operations and financial stability, we need to fortify our existing cybersecurity training programs. This document aims to outline a strategic approach to improve employee cybersecurity awareness and preparedness by Q3 2027.

## Risks and Implications of Retail Cyberattacks

Retail cyberattacks have evolved significantly in complexity and frequency over the past five years. In 2020, data breaches were mostly linked to weak password practices and phishing attacks, now they are increasingly tied to sophisticated ransomware and DDoS attacks. Consequently, our risk management strategies have to constantly evolve to combat these threats effectively.

## Current State of Cybersecurity Training

As we entered Q1 of 2026, our IT resources faced a significant constraint with only 1.8% of our revenue being allocated towards them. This financial limitation led us to make strategic decisions regarding our security measures. Recognizing the need for cost-effective solutions, we decided to focus on enhancing in-house cybersecurity training. This approach allows us to leverage our internal knowledge and expertise, offering a scalable solution that doesn't compromise our protection measures despite the stringent budget.

## Strategies to Enhance Employee Cybersecurity Training

To enhance our cybersecurity training, we've recently partnered with an external security consultant to conduct quarterly audits on our training modules. We have also introduced a new system that tracks the progress of each employee's cybersecurity training. This allows us to identify areas of weakness and tailor our training accordingly.

## Conclusion

By comprehensively examining our current training methods, understanding the evolving risks, and devising effective strategies, we can significantly reduce the danger of potential cyberattacks. This plan, once implemented, should lead to a more resilient digital infrastructure and a safer business environment. It's crucial that we act decisively and promptly to safeguard Lee's Market's future. I recommend that we begin the process of reviewing and revising our cybersecurity training programs immediately.
