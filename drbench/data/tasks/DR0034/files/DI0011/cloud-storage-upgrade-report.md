# Cloud Storage Upgrade Report, May 2025

## Introduction

As part of our ongoing commitment to maintaining robust and secure data management practices, we have recently completed a significant upgrade to our cloud storage limits. This report outlines the process and considerations taken by the Information Security Manager, Jason Wong, during this upgrade. It also explores the implications for data security and future scalability of our cloud-based systems.

## Data Growth and Cloud Storage Needs

During the fiscal year of 2024, Lee's Market saw a 30% increase in data generation from various departments. The sales department, in particular, contributed to 15% of the total data generation, largely due to expanded customer analytics. To effectively manage this data influx, we initiated a comprehensive review of our storage capabilities.

## Security Measures during Cloud Storage Upgrade

In 2023, when we transitioned to a new cloud storage provider, we implemented two-factor authentication and IP whitelisting to strengthen our security framework. Additionally, we conducted regular audits to ensure best practices were upheld and to uncover potential vulnerabilities.

## Post-Upgrade Performance and Scalability

After our cloud storage upgrade in October 2024, we saw an improvement in data retrieval times by 25%. This enhancement has significantly improved our day-to-day operations, particularly in the customer service department, which heavily relies on quick data access.

## Conclusion

The upgrade to our cloud storage limits was executed efficiently, ensuring minimal disruption to our operations. This has not only increased our data capacity, but also reinforced our security measures. Looking forward, we are now better equipped to handle our growing data needs and remain committed to maintaining the highest levels of data security. Future efforts will focus on optimizing performance and further scaling our systems as necessary.
