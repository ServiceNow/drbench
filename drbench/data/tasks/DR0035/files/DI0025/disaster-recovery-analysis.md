# Disaster Recovery Exercise: April 2025 Analysis

## Introduction

As part of our ongoing commitment to ensuring business continuity, we conducted a disaster recovery tabletop exercise in April 2025. This document provides a comprehensive analysis of the exercise, identifying the strengths and weaknesses of our current strategies. The insights gathered from this exercise will help us enhance our disaster recovery plan and improve our response in the event of an actual disaster.

## Overview of the April 2025 Disaster Recovery Exercise

In April 2025, Lee's Market embarked on a significant disaster recovery exercise. The initiative involved multiple departments, including IT, Operations, and Customer Service, to ensure comprehensive system coverage. The objective was to evaluate the robustness of our existing mechanisms to withstand potential disruptions and maintain continuity of business processes.

## Highlights and Key Learnings from the Exercise

The exercise revealed that our team's response time was expedient, with a 98% participation rate across all departments. However, we identified that communication between departments could be improved. Overall, the exercise was insightful, allowing us to understand the strengths and weaknesses of our existing disaster recovery plans.

## Areas of Improvement and Recommendations for Future Exercises

Based on the exercise, we recommend refining our inter-departmental communication during such events. Moreover, we need to conduct these exercises more frequently, possibly on a quarterly basis. We must also incorporate emerging threat scenarios to ensure our preparedness is in line with the evolving risk landscape.

## Conclusion

The April 2025 disaster recovery tabletop exercise provided valuable insights into our current strategies, revealing areas for improvement. Moving forward, we must strengthen our disaster recovery procedures to ensure business continuity. The recommendations detailed in this document should guide our enhancements. It is imperative we continue these exercises regularly to evaluate and improve our disaster response readiness.
