# Leveraging MSSPs for Enhanced Cybersecurity

## Introduction

As the Information Security Manager, I have identified a significant opportunity to enhance Lee's Market's cybersecurity infrastructure through the adoption of managed security service providers (MSSPs). This strategic shift can address our internal resource constraints, while potentially improving threat management, compliance, and customer trust. This document outlines the potential benefits and implementation considerations, and compares this approach with maintaining our traditional in-house cybersecurity measures.

## Addressing Resource Constraints Through MSSPs

In 2024, we faced a significant challenge when two of our primary cybersecurity staff members left Lee's Market. We managed this unexpected resource loss by temporarily increasing our reliance on our MSSP until we could hire and train their replacements. This event underscored the importance of having a flexible MSSP partnership to address resource constraints.

## Strategic Advantages of MSSP Adoption

In June 2025, an internal analysis of our web traffic patterns raised significant cybersecurity concerns, with an alarming 17% of all inbound requests traced back to suspicious IP addresses. This demonstrates a sizeable potential threat vector that could compromise our customer data and operational stability. By leveraging the specialized threat intelligence and resources of a Managed Security Service Provider (MSSP), we can proactively defend against these potential threats more effectively than our current in-house cybersecurity capacity allows. This strategy could not only enhance our threat management capability but also strengthen our compliance posture and reinforce our customers' trust in our ability to protect their data.

## Comparison of MSSP and In-house Cybersecurity Approaches

Throughout 2024, we conducted a comprehensive evaluation to compare the effectiveness of our in-house cybersecurity team and our MSSP. Our internal team excelled at managing day-to-day operations and internal communication, while the MSSP provided a broader range of expertise and round-the-clock monitoring. This comparison helped us better understand the strengths of each approach.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, leveraging MSSPs can address our internal resource constraints and potentially lead to measurable improvements in threat management, compliance, and customer trust. While the transition will require thoughtful planning and execution, the potential benefits could significantly outweigh the costs. Following this analysis, I recommend that we explore potential MSSP partners and initiate a pilot project to assess the viability of this approach.
