# MSSP Adoption for Enhanced Cybersecurity

## Introduction

As Lee's Market continues to expand its digital footprint, ensuring adequate cybersecurity resources has become a top priority. Balancing our growth with the need for secure, trusted platforms for our customers and suppliers is crucial. This report explores the potential benefits of adopting managed security service providers (MSSPs) to address our internal cybersecurity resource constraints, particularly in threat management, compliance, and customer trust. It also contrasts this approach with traditional in-house methods.

## Current State of Lee's Market Cybersecurity Resources

As of May 2025, our evaluation of Lee's Market's cybersecurity measures indicates that there is a significant area for improvement. Specifically, our assessment found that a substantial 35% of the company's systems are yet to implement multi-factor authentication. This raises concern considering the increasing threat landscape in the retail sector, with the high rates of cyberattacks reported. An enhancement of our security measures, therefore, is crucial to ensure the integrity of our operations and the trust of our customers.

## MSSP Capabilities and Potential Advantages

In general, Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs) can offer a wide range of benefits. They can provide around-the-clock security monitoring, manage incident response and recovery, and offer expertise in various areas of cybersecurity. As of Q1 2025, approximately 60% of North American enterprises have adopted MSSPs for their cybersecurity needs.

## Impact on Compliance, Threat Management, and Customer Trust

Cybersecurity measures significantly impact customer trust and regulatory compliance. According to a 2024 study, nearly 80% of customers are more likely to do business with companies that prioritize data security. Similarly, organizations that fail to meet stringent compliance standards can face hefty fines, with 40% of businesses experiencing at least one compliance-related incident in 2024.

## Conclusion

The adoption of MSSPs could be a pivotal step in addressing our cybersecurity challenges, providing potential improvements in threat management and compliance. Not only would this offer an effective solution to our resource constraints, but it could also significantly enhance customer trust in our digital platforms. The next step would be to identify suitable MSSP partners, assess their capabilities, and develop an implementation strategy for seamless integration with our existing systems.
