# MSSP Adoption for Enhanced Cybersecurity

## Introduction

As we expand our retail footprint and digital services, Lee's Market's cybersecurity efforts must evolve to protect our business, customer trust, and regulatory compliance. This report explores the potential benefits and implementation considerations of outsourcing some cybersecurity functions to Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs). We will consider how such a pivot might alleviate our internal resource constraints and improve threat management. The report will also compare the effectiveness of MSSPs against traditional in-house cybersecurity approaches.

## MSSP Efficacy in Threat Management and Compliance

In 2023, we trialed an MSSP solution to monitor supplier portal activities. Despite the initial promise, we faced challenges in integrating the solution with our existing IT systems. However, the MSSP provided a 30% improvement in threat detection within the trial period.

## Customer Perception and Trust Following MSSP Adoption

A 2024 customer survey showed that 70% of our customers were unaware of the role MSSPs play in securing their data. Our efforts to educate them about MSSPs' role in protecting their data led to a 15% increase in customer trust. Interestingly, 65% of the respondents were more concerned about product quality than data security.

## Operational Impact of MSSP Integration

Our cybersecurity monitoring capabilities in April 2025 were significantly constrained due to resource limitations, as we had only two full-time equivalent employees dedicated to this critical aspect of our security framework. This severely limited our capacity to provide round-the-clock surveillance, leaving us vulnerable during off-peak hours. By integrating managed security service providers (MSSPs) into our security infrastructure, we can alleviate this resource burden and ensure continuous cybersecurity monitoring, bolstering our defensive capabilities against the heightened cyber threat landscape in the retail sector.

## Conclusion

The adoption of MSSPs can potentially transform our cybersecurity approach, freeing internal resources and enhancing our threat management capabilities. It is crucial to ensure a smooth transition that minimally impacts operations while maximizing security enhancements. Our customers' trust in our data protection measures may also see significant improvement. The next step would be to engage multiple MSSPs for initial discussions and assess their potential fit with our specific security needs and business model.
