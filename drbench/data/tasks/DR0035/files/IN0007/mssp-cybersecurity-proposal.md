# Leveraging MSSPs for Enhanced Cybersecurity

## Introduction

As Lee's Market continues to expand its digital footprint, it is essential that we constantly evaluate and improve our cybersecurity strategies. Our current in-house resources are stretched, and it's time to consider how partnering with Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs) can mitigate this constraint. This document explores the potential benefits and impact of this approach on our threat management, compliance and customer trust between Q1 and Q4 2025.

## Assessing Potential MSSPs for Strategic Partnership

Over the past two years, we have seen a rise in MSSPs offering specialized services in the retail sector. Some of these providers have shown impressive performance in terms of incident response times and threat detection capabilities. However, our experience with third-party vendors in other IT areas has led us to exercise caution when considering new partnerships.

## Impact on Threat Management, Compliance, and Customer Trust

The trust of our customers is paramount, and we have seen that a strong cybersecurity posture can positively influence that trust. According to our most recent customer survey in November 2024, 82% of customers expressed increased confidence in our brand after we publicly addressed our cybersecurity enhancements. Ensuring we maintain this level of trust is a key focus for our department.

## Current State of Cybersecurity Resource Constraints

From March to June 2025, our team at Lee's Market had the challenging task of incorporating the requirements of three fresh regulatory mandates into our data privacy framework. These additions significantly stretched our existing resources, revealing the limitations of our in-house cybersecurity team. This situation underscored the need for supplementary support, particularly in an industry increasingly targeted by cyber threats. As such, the transition towards Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs) could bolster our defensive capabilities, ensuring we stay ahead of the curve in a rapidly evolving threat landscape.

## Conclusion

Adopting MSSPs could significantly reduce our internal resource constraints and potentially enhance our threat management and compliance capabilities. This approach may also increase customer trust by demonstrating our commitment to securing their data. It's recommended we identify potential MSSPs for further evaluation and devise a plan for a seamless transition. Stakeholders will be kept informed of progress and key decisions throughout this process.
