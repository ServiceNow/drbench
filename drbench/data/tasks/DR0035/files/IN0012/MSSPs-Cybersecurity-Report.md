# Leveraging MSSPs for Enhanced Cybersecurity

## Introduction

As Lee's Market continues to expand its digital footprint, safeguarding our digital and operational infrastructure becomes increasingly critical. Balancing our internal resources to meet cybersecurity needs is a significant challenge. This report explores the potential of adopting Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs) to bolster our cybersecurity capabilities, manage threats and compliance, and strengthen customer trust, as well as the projected impacts compared to maintaining our current in-house approach.

## Assessing Current Cybersecurity Resource Constraints

An analysis of our internal threat intelligence capacity as of April 2025 revealed some critical limitations. Our internal security team was aware of only 40% of new threats that emerged in the retail sector within this period. This gap in threat awareness underscores our existing cybersecurity resource constraints and highlights the pressing need for bolstered security measures. Engaging with Managed Security Service Providers (MSSPs) could be instrumental in addressing these shortcomings, by providing us access to dedicated resources and up-to-the-minute threat intelligence.

## Exploring Managed Security Service Providers' Potential

In 2023, we conducted a preliminary assessment of three leading MSSPs but decided to continue with our in-house security solutions. The primary reasons were the high transition costs and the potential risks associated with outsourcing sensitive data management. However, we did recognize the advanced threat intelligence and response capabilities offered by these providers.

## Projected Impact on Threat Management, Compliance, and Customer Trust

Over the past two years, we have seen a steady increase in customer awareness and concern about data privacy. In 2024, we conducted a customer survey, and 70% of respondents stated that they would likely shop more frequently at a store that prioritizes data protection. This trend aligns with a broader industry shift towards prioritizing cybersecurity measures in the retail sector.

## Conclusion

Adopting MSSPs could potentially address our internal resource constraints and enhance our overall cybersecurity posture. A well-managed transition can improve threat management, ensure robust compliance, and solidify customer trust. Understanding our current limitations and the potential advantages MSSPs offer is vital to making an informed decision. Further analysis and careful vendor selection will be crucial for successful implementation.
