# Revamping Loyalty Program: Multi-Generational Approach

## Introduction

As Lee's Market strives to improve our customer experience and engagement, we are considering a redesign of our loyalty program. The new design aims to meet the evolving digital preferences of Gen Z and Millennial customers while maintaining the traditional service expectations of Gen X and Boomer customers. This document will explore key considerations in achieving this balance, ensuring our program remains effective and beneficial for all customers. The focus will be on understanding customer preferences, integrating technology, and maintaining personal touch.

## Understanding Diverse Customer Preferences

Our pilot test, scheduled for Q4 2025 through Q1 2026, is designed to evaluate the impact of hybrid loyalty rewards on different customer generations at Lee's Market. The preliminary projections from this initiative indicate a promising enhancement in customer satisfaction, as measured by the Net Promoter Score (NPS). It is anticipated that we will see an uplift of eight NPS points amongst our Gen Z and Millennial customers. Meanwhile, our Gen X and Boomer customers are expected to show a favorable response as well, with a predicted NPS rise of five points. These figures underscore the potential of the hybrid model in meeting the diverse customer experience expectations for 2026.

## Integrating Technology for Enhanced Engagement

In the last quarter of 2025, we deployed a new POS system across all our stores, resulting in a 15% increase in transaction efficiency. We also implemented a mobile app, which has been downloaded by 30% of our regular customers. Our upcoming plans include experimenting with AI-driven recommendations and AR-powered shopping experiences.

## Maintaining Personal Connection in Digital Environment

Despite the shift towards digital, our in-store customer service remains a key differentiator for Lee's Market. We have a team of 200 dedicated customer service representatives who ensure that our customers receive personalized service. Their efforts have led to an overall customer satisfaction rate of 85%.

## Conclusion

Successfully redesigning our loyalty program requires a nuanced understanding of our diverse customer base and their preferences. Leveraging technology can increase engagement with younger customers without alienating older ones. It's crucial that we maintain the personal connection and service quality that our customers value. As we move forward, we need to ensure our approach is flexible and adaptable to continue serving our customers effectively.
