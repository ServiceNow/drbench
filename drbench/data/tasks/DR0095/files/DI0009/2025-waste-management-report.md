# 2025 Waste Management Report - Lee's Market

## Introduction

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Lee's Market's waste management practices for the year 2025. It critically examines the waste output, with a focus on the percentage of waste that was recycled or composted. This data is essential to understand the sustainability initiatives of our company and contributes towards improving our environmental footprint.

## Overview of 2025 Total Waste Output

In 2025, Lee's Market experienced a significant increase in waste output due to the surge in customer traffic. A major contributor to this increase was the packaging waste, comprising an estimated 30% of the total waste. Additionally, food waste from perishable products accounted for about 20% of the total waste output.

## Analysis of Recycled and Composted Waste

Our analysis revealed that paper and cardboard were the most recycled materials in 2025, contributing to a large portion of our overall recycling volume. The composting sector primarily dealt with organic waste from our fresh produce and bakery sections. However, there was a notable challenge in recycling plastic waste due to its varied forms and complexities.

## Efficiency of Waste Management Practices

Lee's Market invested heavily in enhancing its waste management practices in 2025. The use of waste compaction technology improved our waste handling capacity by 40%. Our partnership with local waste management service providers also assured timely waste disposal, reducing the chances of waste pile-up at our stores.

## Conclusion

The report reveals significant insights into Lee's Market's waste management practices. The detailed analysis of waste output and recycling/composting initiatives shows the extent of our sustainable practices. These findings can assist in strategizing further sustainability initiatives and improving our waste management practices. Let's continue to work towards reducing our environmental impact and enhancing our corporate responsibility.
