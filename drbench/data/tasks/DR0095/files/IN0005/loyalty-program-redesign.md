# Loyalty Program Redesign: Meeting Diverse Customer Needs

## Introduction

As Lee's Market continues to grow and adapt, our customer engagement strategies must evolve in tandem. This report aims to outline the key considerations for redesigning our loyalty program to cater to the distinct preferences of both our younger and older customer bases. We will explore the current market trends, technological advancements, and customers' expectations to create a program that effectively engages all demographics.

## Current Market Trends in Customer Loyalty Programs

The recent analysis of our customer data reveals an interesting trend. It is evident that our digital-first customers, predominantly app users, exhibit a higher customer lifetime value (CLV) averaging at $1,800 as of the fourth quarter in 2025. This contrasts with the traditional card/coupon user base, which maintains a CLV of around $1,350. These trends justify our strategic plan to invest in enhancing mobile personalization features, while simultaneously continuing to support our legacy formats to cater to the needs of our traditional, yet high-value customers.

## Balancing Digital Innovation with Traditional Service

As of Q4 2025, many retail companies are making significant investments in digital transformation while maintaining traditional service offerings. For example, some have introduced self-checkout options, but have also increased staffing in customer service departments to ensure a personal touch. It's a delicate balance to strike, but one that is becoming increasingly necessary in today's retail landscape.

## Meeting the Expectations of Diverse Demographics

In recent years, we've seen retailers attempting to cater to a wider demographic base. For instance, some have introduced product lines tailored to specific age groups, while others have focused on providing multilingual support to accommodate Richmond's diverse population. Between 2019 and 2024, retailers serving diverse demographics have seen a 15% increase in customer satisfaction rates.

## Conclusion

Redesigning Lee's Market's loyalty program requires a careful balance between embracing digital trends and maintaining traditional service values. Understanding the preferences of our diverse customer base will be instrumental in creating a program that resonates with all. Moving forward, the insights gathered in this report should guide our redesign process to ensure we continue to foster strong customer relationships and enhance our market position.
