# Impact of US Tariffs on Lee's Market Sales

## Introduction

The imposition of US tariffs in 2025 has caused a notable downturn in Canadian retail sales. As a result, Lee's Market must explore product-level sales trends across various categories to revise our sales forecasts for 2027. This document aims to uncover insights that will aid in making informed decisions and strategies moving forward.

## Analysis of Tariff-Exposed Category Sales

Our analysis of the top 50 tariff-impacted SKUs within Lee's Market from Q4 2025 to Q4 2026 reveals significant insights. The elasticity coefficient, a key indicator of sales volume sensitivity to price alterations, was calculated to be around -1.25. This points to the potential for careful modelling of promotional depth against anticipated profit or revenue reduction. By focusing on SKUs with mid-range elasticity, we can optimize promotional frequency, setting discount thresholds that maintain margins, and more accurately forecast sales within tariff-exposed categories.

## Assessment of Tariff-Insulated Category Performance

The grocery department, widely considered tariff-insulated, has been a consistent performer for Lee's Market. In 2024, the department accounted for 40% of the company's total annual sales. The fresh produce and bakery sections were the top contributors to the department's revenue.

## Revision of Sales Forecasts Considering Tariff Impacts

Sales forecasting at Lee's Market involves multiple departments, including sales, finance, and operations. The process begins in the first quarter of each fiscal year, with sales data from the previous years serving as the basis for projections. The forecasts are then revised quarterly based on the actual sales performance.

## Conclusion

The insights drawn from this analysis suggest that tariff-exposed and tariff-insulated categories have varying impacts on our sales. As a result, we need to adjust our sales forecasts accordingly for 2027. These findings will help us strategize for the future and mitigate any potential risks. It is crucial that these insights are thoroughly considered in our future planning and decision-making process.
