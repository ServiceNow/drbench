# Tariff Impact Analysis and Sales Forecast

## Introduction

In light of recent US tariffs, it is crucial for Lee's Market to understand its impact on our sales trends. This document presents a detailed analysis of our product-level sales across tariff-exposed and tariff-insulated categories. The insights derived will guide us in updating our sales forecasts for 2027. This information is crucial for our strategic planning and decision-making processes.

## Overview of Tariff-Exposed Product Sales

As we delve into the third quarter of 2025 sales data, it's evident that Lee's Market's inventory is significantly impacted by the import tariffs, with nearly 43% of our stock-keeping units (SKUs) by value originating from the U.S. and other tariff-affected regions. This considerable percentage underscores our dependency on these imports, laying bare our vulnerability to tariff fluctuations. It is critical to use this insight when forecasting sales for the coming years, considering potential shifts in price sensitivity among our customers. This information can also guide strategies in SKU segmentation, possibly prompting a lean towards domestic, low-tariff exposure goods for future markdown initiatives or product substitutions.

## Analysis of Tariff-Insulated Product Performance

Tariff-insulated products, primarily locally sourced or imported from non-US regions, have always been a strong component of our sales strategy. In 2023, these products accounted for approximately 40% of our total sales. Additionally, the sales of these goods grew by an average of 5% year by year from 2020 to 2023.

## Sales Forecast Adjustments for 2027

Sales forecasting involves a meticulous analysis of past sales data, market conditions, and business trends. At Lee's Market, the finance department collaborates with sales and marketing teams to prepare these forecasts. In 2026, we implemented a new software tool to streamline this process and improve accuracy.

## Conclusion

The recent tariffs have significantly influenced our sales, particularly in the tariff-exposed categories. By analysing these trends, we've made necessary adjustments to our 2027 sales forecast. It is recommended that we focus on tariff-insulated categories and explore new markets to mitigate risks. Continuous monitoring of the situation and flexibility in our strategies will be key to our success.
