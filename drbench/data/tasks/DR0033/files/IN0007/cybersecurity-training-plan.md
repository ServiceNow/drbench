# Cybersecurity Awareness and Training Plan

## Introduction

As Lee's Market continues to grow across the US and Canadian markets, the need for a robust cybersecurity awareness and training program has never been more critical. The continuous high employee turnover and seasonal hiring pose significant risks to our digital and operational infrastructure. This document outlines our strategy to mitigate these risks, minimize incidents caused by human error, and support the company's growth.

## Assessing Current Cybersecurity Knowledge and Procedures

Our analysis of incident reports from August 2024 to August 2025 reveals that Lee's Market's operations were temporarily disrupted for a collective duration of 17 hours due to cyber-related incidents. A significant portion of this disruption time, equivalent to 14 hours, was traced back to incidents induced by human error. This is a clear indication that our current cybersecurity knowledge, as well as the protocols in place, need to be re-evaluated and enhanced to reduce the vulnerability of our systems and safeguard our business operations.

## Designing a Scalable Cybersecurity Training Program

In the past, we've collaborated with leading cybersecurity firms to develop our training modules. These modules were primarily tailored to the needs of our IT department, focusing on network security, malware detection, and data encryption techniques. This approach has significantly improved our overall system security, as evidenced by a 20% reduction in detected threats in 2023 compared to 2022.

## Implementation and Continuous Improvement of Security Measures

Our commitment to continuous improvement of security measures is demonstrated by our quarterly security audits. These audits, carried out by third-party cybersecurity firms, assess our vulnerability to different types of cyberattacks. Based on the findings, we then fine-tune our security protocols to ensure the highest level of data protection for our customers and suppliers.

## Conclusion

This cybersecurity awareness and training plan is designed to ensure the protection of Lee's Market's digital and operational infrastructure. By assessing our current procedures, designing a comprehensive training program, and focusing on continuous improvement, we can mitigate risks associated with high employee turnover and seasonal hiring. The next step is to gather key stakeholders for a detailed review of this plan and its implementation. Together, we can make Lee's Market a safer place to work and shop.
