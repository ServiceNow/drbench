# Q3 2025 Produce Sourcing Improvement Report

## Introduction

This report explores the strategies employed by Jason Wong in Q3 2025 to enhance Lee's Market's local produce sourcing using staff and supplier feedback. It aims to provide insights into the effective approaches and their impact on our supply chain operations. The document will be beneficial for departments related to procurement, operations, and supply chain management.

## Analysis of Staff Feedback on Local Sourcing

In Q2 2025, we conducted a survey among our staff to gather their thoughts on our local sourcing efforts. 78% of respondents indicated that they strongly supported the initiative owing to the potential positive impacts on the local economy. Additionally, 64% believed it would enhance the freshness and quality of our product offerings, contributing to an improved customer experience.

## Supplier Perspective on Sourcing Improvements

In June 2025, we initiated a dialogue with our suppliers to discuss potential improvements to our sourcing process. Many suppliers expressed a desire for a more streamlined communication channel and better forecast accuracy to improve inventory management. We are currently exploring technological solutions to address these concerns.

## Impact of Feedback-Driven Changes on Sourcing

Following the implementation of feedback-driven changes in Q4 2025, we have seen a 12% increase in the number of local suppliers. We believe that these changes have the potential to significantly enhance our product diversity and availability. We are also optimistic about the potential for improved relationships with our local suppliers.

## Conclusion

The report elucidates how staff and supplier feedback can be leveraged to improve local produce sourcing. Jason Wong's approach has yielded significant improvements in our supply chain, demonstrating the value of feedback-oriented strategies. We should consider further incorporating staff and supplier feedback in our strategic decision-making. Future studies could explore other areas of operation where such an approach could be beneficial.
