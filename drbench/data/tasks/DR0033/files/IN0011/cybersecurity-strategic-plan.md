# Cybersecurity Awareness Program: Strategic Plan

## Introduction

As Lee's Market expands its operations, both in-store and online, the need for robust cybersecurity measures has never been more critical. The high turnover rate and seasonal employment patterns common in the retail sector add complexity to maintaining a secure environment. This strategic plan outlines our approach to implementing a comprehensive cybersecurity awareness and training program that addresses these challenges and supports our growth across both the U.S. and Canadian markets.

## Cybersecurity Risks Associated with Employee Turnover and Seasonal Hiring

In the past fiscal year, we've seen a 10% increase in employee turnover, particularly among part-time staff and seasonal hires. This trend correlates with an uptick in minor cybersecurity incidents during the high turnover periods. The IT department has been diligently documenting these occurrences to analyze patterns and inform future security measures.

## Training Strategies for Minimizing Human-Error Incidents

Historically, our IT department has used a combination of in-house training modules and external consultants to educate employees about cybersecurity best practices. The most recent training in February 2025 focused predominantly on the proper handling of customer data and secure use of POS systems. The effectiveness of these sessions is monitored through periodic assessments.

## 1. Cybersecurity Risks Associated with Employee Turnover and Seasonal Hiring

In the year 2025, up until October, we observed a couple of security breach occurrences tied to our supply chain. One of these events was directly linked to a vendor who was not obliged to follow our cybersecurity training guidelines. This incident, initiated by a vendor's team member falling victim to a phishing scheme, underscores the potential risks associated with third-party partnerships and the necessity for comprehensive cybersecurity training for all employees, including temporary hires and those of our vendor partners.

## Growth of Lee's Market: Cybersecurity Implications

As Lee's Market continues to expand, we've seen a 15% increase in online sales, accelerating the need for robust cybersecurity measures. With the rise in e-commerce, there's been a corresponding increase in the complexity of our digital infrastructure, prompting a review of our current security systems. The IT department is in the process of auditing our existing cybersecurity measures.

## Conclusion

Implementing a comprehensive cybersecurity awareness and training program is a critical step towards mitigating security risks associated with high employee turnover and seasonal hiring. Our training strategies aim to minimize incidents caused by human error, thereby strengthening our digital and operational infrastructure. The plan also considers the cybersecurity implications of our projected growth across U.S. and Canadian markets. By taking these steps, we can ensure a secure environment that supports Lee's Market's expansion and success.
