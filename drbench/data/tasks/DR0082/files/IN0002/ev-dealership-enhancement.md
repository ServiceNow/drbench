# Enhancing EV Dealership Showroom Visibility

## Introduction

As part of our ongoing initiatives to bolster Elexion Automotive's market presence, this report will explore strategies to enhance our retail dealership presence. The focus will be to increase our electric vehicle (EV) showroom visibility specifically in the fourth quarter. This document will provide insights on industry trends, competitive analysis, and potential partnerships.

## Review of Competitor Dealership Strategies

Our competitors have been aggressive in their dealership strategies. For instance, in Q3, Zephyr EVs launched a new dealership design that focuses on interactive displays. Meanwhile, Bolt Motors introduced an augmented reality experience in their showrooms. Both strategies have increased their brand visibility, but it's still early to assess the long-term effects.

## Potential Partnership Opportunities for Showroom Exposure

In the EV industry, partnerships with energy companies are becoming increasingly common. For instance, PowerGrid Inc. recently partnered with a leading EV manufacturer to offer exclusive charging solutions. Other companies are exploring collaborations with tech giants for in-car entertainment systems. These partnerships can potentially increase brand recognition and customer trust.

## Analyzing Current EV Showroom Performances

In our efforts to optimize Elexion's retail dealership presence, a deep dive into our service ticket data revealed some critical patterns. A significant 35% of these tickets necessitated escalation, pinpointing a gap in our pre-visit troubleshooting efforts. This lack of efficient remote diagnostics is not only impacting our dealership service operations but also potentially influencing our showroom visibility. An improved remote diagnostic process could reduce the need for physical service center visits, a strategy that has proven successful for our competitors, thereby freeing up valuable showroom space for showcasing our cutting-edge EVs.
