# Impact of EV Trends on Global Dynamics

## Introduction

As our Competitive Intelligence team continues to monitor the evolving landscape of electric vehicles (EVs), certain developments warrant our collective attention. This report delves into the 2024 'Trends in electric cars' by iea.org, focusing on the anticipated changes in battery technology and EV supply chains. These changes are predicted to significantly impact global production and delivery dynamics by Q2 2026. Our understanding and response to these trends are crucial in maintaining Elexion's competitive edge in the EV market.

## Shifts in EV Supply Chain Landscapes

During Q2 2023, Elexion Automotive's procurement team introduced a new supplier evaluation process to ensure compliance with our sustainability goals. The process includes assessing the environmental impact of suppliers' manufacturing processes, which has led to a 20% reduction in our carbon footprint. We are also working on reducing the lead time for acquiring key components to improve our production efficiency.

## Emerging Battery Technologies and Their Implications

As we navigate the rapidly evolving global EV landscape, our internal production planning data for the first quarter of 2026 reveals a strategic prioritization of different battery technologies. Lithium-ion batteries, due to their proven efficiency and reliability, are projected to account for the lion's share at 80% of our electric vehicle allocations. However, we are also proactively investing in emerging technologies, with solid-state batteries forming 15% of our production plan, and sodium-ion batteries making up the remaining 5%. This distribution reflects our commitment to staying abreast of cutting-edge technology, while also maintaining our robust production capabilities.

## Projected Impact on Global Production and Delivery

In 2023, Elexion Automotive's global production output increased by 12% compared to the previous year. This growth was largely driven by the introduction of our new model, which was well-received by customers. Our delivery process was also optimized, reducing delivery times by 25% on average.

## Conclusion

Given the projected developments in battery technology and shifts in EV supply chains, we anticipate significant changes in global production and delivery dynamics. These trends provide both challenges and opportunities for Elexion Automotive. Moving forward, we need to strategically align our business decisions to these insights, ensuring our continued competitiveness in the evolving EV landscape. Further discussions will be necessary to determine the specific steps our cross-functional teams will need to undertake.
