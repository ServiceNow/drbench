# ZEV Mandate Compliance Framework Update

## Introduction

As a proactive response to Canada's revised Zero Emission Vehicle (ZEV) mandate, Elexion Automotive is taking steps to update its compliance documentation framework. This document provides an outline for our approach towards ensuring the verification and recording of supply-chain investment credits under the new regulation by 2026. The guidance herein will support our commitment to regulatory compliance while optimizing our position in the electric vehicle market.

## Implications for Supply-Chain Investment Credits

The revised ZEV mandate has implications for the automotive industry's supply chain. Companies can receive investment credits for investing in sustainable supply chain practices. These credits can then be used to offset other costs associated with the production of electric vehicles.

## Overview of Canada's Revised ZEV Mandate

As we move toward 2026, Elexion Automotive is making pivotal strides to conform to Canada's updated ZEV mandate. An integral part of this plan is the enhancement of our compliance documentation framework. Once the changes are rolled out, we anticipate a significant improvement in the accuracy of our documentation and verification processes. By the final quarter of 2026, we project a decline of approximately 35% in discrepancies and errors in our compliance documentation. This projection is a testament to the efficiency improvements expected from system upgrades and supplier standardization.

## Proposed Framework for Compliance Documentation

A robust compliance documentation framework is crucial for automotive companies in the electric vehicle industry. It ensures that all regulatory requirements are met and reduces the risk of non-compliance. The framework should be updated regularly to reflect changes in regulations and industry standards.

## Conclusion

By understanding the implications of Canada's revised ZEV mandate, Elexion Automotive can effectively strategize its compliance efforts. Continuous review and updating of this framework is recommended to keep pace with evolving regulations.
