# 2025 Model Battery Charging Efficiency Report

## Introduction

The document outlines a comprehensive review of the battery charging efficiency for Elexion Automotive's 2025 model vehicles as observed during the October 2024 testing phase. The purpose of this report is to evaluate the performance of our electric vehicles' battery systems, which are integral to our commitment to sustainability and user-centric technology. Insights from this document will aid in identifying areas of improvement and potential risks, ensuring that our electric vehicles continue to meet the high standards of performance and sustainability expected by our customers.

## Testing Procedures and Quality Assurance Measures

Elexion Automotive has always been committed to stringent testing and quality assurance measures. In September 2024, the company implemented a revised testing protocol which included a new series of stress tests for the vehicle's powertrain, in addition to our traditional safety and performance checks. The Quality Assurance department also introduced a new compliance matrix to ensure adherence to cross-border regulations.

## Detailed Analysis of Battery Charging Efficiency

Our engineers have been exploring various factors affecting battery charging efficiency. In 2023, we conducted a comprehensive investigation into the effects of ambient temperature on battery performance, determining that optimal charging efficiency was achieved at 20°C. We also found that charging efficiency decreases by 0.8% for each 1°C deviation from this optimal temperature.

## Implications and Recommendations for Battery Technology

Given the rapid advancements in battery technology and increasing customer expectations, it is essential for Elexion Automotive to continually innovate. Based on our 2023 battery performance analysis, we recommended an investment of $40 million for a new battery research and development facility. This move aims to further enhance the energy efficiency and lifespan of Elexion's electric vehicle batteries.

## Conclusion

The testing procedures in October 2024 provided valuable insights into the battery charging efficiency of our 2025 model vehicles. The results, detailed in this report, highlight areas of strength and potential improvements for our battery technology. It is suggested that the team further investigates the identified areas for improvement to enhance our electric vehicle performance. Continued focus on battery efficiency will contribute significantly to our mission of accelerating the transition to zero-emission transportation.
